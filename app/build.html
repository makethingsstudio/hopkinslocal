BuildLocal

Expanding opportunities for local companies and residents in design and construction

HopkinsLocal will increase participation of local businesses; Minority, Women and Disadvantaged Business Enterprises; and city residents in design, consultant, and construction opportunities related to capital projects.

The program will:

  • Require 17 percent of Johns Hopkins’ portfolio of construction contracts be filled by Minority, Women and Disadvantages Business Enterprises (MBE), and raise that requirement to 20 percent by FY2019
  • Begin tracking participation by Local Business Enterprises in order to set requirements in future years.
  • Include a liquidated damages clause for contractors that do not meet stated goals for engaging MBEs as suppliers or subcontractors
  • Implement a local hiring policy to require contractors to make a good faith effort to hire local residents for new jobs that result from construction projects
  • Offer a prequalification process to help contractors and consultants work effectively with the university and health system

About

HopkinsLocal

Build. Hire. Buy

HopkinsLocal is an initiative to build a stronger Baltimore by promoting economic growth. It builds on Johns Hopkins University and Health System’s role as an anchor institution for the city by laying out a plan to look to the city when it builds, hires and buys.

Hopkins Local is a firm and measurable commitment to increase design and construction contracts with local, minority-owned, and women-owned businesses, expand the number of new hires that come from city neighborhoods, and spend more purchasing dollars with city vendors. The initiative will also support ongoing efforts to support diversity in Johns Hopkins’ workforce and among our business partners.

With campuses across the city and more employees than any other private employer in Maryland, Johns Hopkins University and Health System can leverage its economic activity to help make Baltimore a place where people are proud to live, work, and study.
