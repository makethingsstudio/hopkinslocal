HireLocal

Expanding employment opportunities at Johns Hopkins for city residents

HopkinsLocal will increase the number of city residents that are employed at Johns Hopkins and help a diverse workforce pursue successful careers in the university and health system.

The program will:

  • Ensure that 40 percent of new hires for a group of targeted entry-level positions are from Baltimore neighborhoods in selected zip codes by 2018.
  • Strengthen the recruitment of qualified and competitive local candidates at all levels
  • Support the growth and retention of local and underrepresented employees at the University and Health System.
  • Expand partnerships with organizations that help identify and prepare individuals for careers with the university and health system.

About

HopkinsLocal

Build. Hire. Buy

HopkinsLocal is an initiative to build a stronger Baltimore by promoting economic growth. It builds on Johns Hopkins University and Health System’s role as an anchor institution for the city by laying out a plan to look to the city when it builds, hires and buys.

Hopkins Local is a firm and measurable commitment to increase design and construction contracts with local, minority-owned, and women-owned businesses, expand the number of new hires that come from city neighborhoods, and spend more purchasing dollars with city vendors. The initiative will also support ongoing efforts to support diversity in Johns Hopkins’ workforce and among our business partners.

With campuses across the city and more employees than any other private employer in Maryland, Johns Hopkins University and Health System can leverage its economic activity to help make Baltimore a place where people are proud to live, work, and study.
