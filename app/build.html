Expanding opportunities for local contractors and vendors, and minority, women, and disadvantaged business enterprises.

Building Baltimore Together

To increase participation of Minority, Women and Disadvantaged Business Enterprises (MBEs) and Local Business Enterprises (LBEs) in design, consultant and construction opportunities related to capital projects.

  • Implement a local hiring policy for design & construction projects.
  • Include MBE and LBE requirements in the contracts for construction projects.
  • Foster the capabilities and skill level of those MBE and LBE firms.
  • Include a liquidated damages clause for contractors that do not meet stated goals for engaging MBEs as suppliers or subcontractors. This clause is a key element that sets Johns Hopkins’ program apart from others.
  • Develop a prequalification process to help contractors and consultants be prepared to work with the university and health system.
  • Complete a website to display a list of construction projects up for bid and outline the process for businesses to become prequalified.

About

HopkinsLocal

Build. Hire. Buy

HopkinsLocal is an initiative to build a stronger Baltimore by promoting economic growth. It builds on Johns Hopkins University and Health System’s role as an anchor institution for the city by laying out a plan to look to the city when it builds, hires and buys.

Hopkins Local is a firm and measurable commitment to increase design and construction contracts with local, minority-owned, and women-owned businesses, expand the number of new hires that come from city neighborhoods, and spend more purchasing dollars with city vendors. The initiative will also support ongoing efforts to support diversity in Johns Hopkins’ workforce and among our business partners.

With campuses across the city and more employees than any other private employer in Maryland, Johns Hopkins University and Health System can leverage its economic activity to help make Baltimore a place where people are proud to live, work, and study.
